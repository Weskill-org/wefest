## SEO OPTIMIZATION RULES

### Keywords
- Primary keyword: 1.5% density (75 occurrences in 5000 words)
- Use in: H1, first 100 words, 5-7 H2 headings, conclusion, meta, URL
- Secondary keywords (3-4): 1% density each
- LSI keywords: 250+ natural mentions (event management, sponsorship acquisition, ticketing, student engagement, fest planning, vendor coordination, budget allocation, etc.)
- WeFest brand mentions: 20-25 times throughout

### Title Optimization
Format: [Number/Power Word] [Keyword] [Benefit/Promise] | WeFest
Examples:
- "Complete College Fest Sponsorship Guide: Acquire Funding in 30 Days | WeFest"
- "15 Proven Event Management Strategies That Transform College Fests | WeFest"
- "Digital Ticketing Mastery: Ultimate Platform Guide for Campus Events | WeFest"
Length: 50-60 characters
Include: Primary keyword, power word (Complete/Ultimate/Proven/Essential), benefit

### Meta Description
- 150-160 characters
- Primary keyword in first 120 chars
- Include WeFest brand
- Clear value + CTA
Example: "Master college fest management with proven strategies for sponsorship, ticketing & coordination. WeFest's platform simplifies every step. Start free today!"

### URL Structure
Format: /blog/[primary-keyword-3-5-words]
Examples: /college-fest-sponsorship-guide, /event-ticketing-platform-features
Rules: Lowercase, hyphens, no stop words, keyword-rich

### Heading Hierarchy
- **H1:** One only (article title)
- **H2:** 8-10 sections (keyword variations in 60%)
- **H3:** 3-5 per H2 section (long-tail keywords)
- **H4:** Sparingly for deep details
- Every heading provides clear value preview

### Internal Linking (15-20 total)
Distribution:
- Introduction: 2 links
- Main sections (3-5): 3 links each
- Conclusion: 3-4 links

Link to:
- Pillar content (2-3 times): "Complete Guide to..."
- Related clusters (6-8 times): specific topic deep-dives
- WeFest features (3-4 times): platform capabilities
- Resources (2-3 times): templates, checklists

Anchor text examples:
- "Learn advanced sponsorship proposal techniques"
- "Explore WeFest's integrated ticketing system"
- "Discover budget planning strategies for college events"
- "Master vendor coordination with our comprehensive guide"

### External Linking (5-8 total)
- Link to: .edu research, .gov statistics, industry authorities (EventBrite, Cvent insights)
- Recent sources (no date restrictions, focus on authority)
- Relevant context supporting your points
- Use rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank"

## WRITING STYLE GUIDE

### Tone & Voice
- **Energetic:** Dynamic verbs (transform, revolutionize, optimize, maximize)
- **Empowering:** "You can achieve...", "Your fest deserves...", "Take control of..."
- **Professional:** Data-driven, authoritative, credible
- **Relatable:** Address real organizer pain points directly
- **Solution-focused:** Every problem gets actionable answer

### Sentence Construction
- Average length: 15-20 words
- Mix: Short punchy (8-10), medium (15-20), longer complex (25-30)
- Active voice: 85%+ ("WeFest enables teams" not "Teams are enabled")
- Transition words: 30%+ (however, therefore, additionally, consequently)

### Paragraph Rules
- Length: 2-4 sentences (80-150 words max)
- One main idea per paragraph
- White space between all paragraphs
- Start with topic sentence, end with transition

### Engagement Techniques
- Direct address: "You, your, you're"
- Rhetorical questions: "What if you could...", "Why settle for..."
- Power words: Proven, comprehensive, essential, ultimate, revolutionary
- Specific numbers: "Increase by 3x" not "significant increase"
- Mini case examples: "When XYZ College implemented..."
- Pull quotes: Highlight key statistics or insights

### Content Quality Standards
- Reading level: 8th-9th grade (Flesch Reading Ease: 65-75)
- Zero fluff - every sentence adds value
- Actionable insights in every section
- Balance theory with practical application
- Include specific examples from college fest context
